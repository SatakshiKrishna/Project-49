Origami(Japanese pronunciation: [oɾiɡami] or [oɾiꜜɡami], from ori meaning "fulding", and kami meaning "paper" (kami changes to gami due to rendaku)) is the art of paper fulding, which is often associated with Japanese culture. In modern usage, the word "origami" is used as an inclusive term for all fulding practices, regardless of their culture of origin. The goal is to transform a flat square sheet of paper into a finished sculpture through fulding and sculpting techniques. Modern origami practitioners generally discourage the use of cuts, glue, or markings on the paper. Origami fulders often use the Japanese word kirigami to refer to designs which use cuts.
